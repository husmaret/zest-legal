Zest Privacy Policy

Effective date: 23 May 2026 · Last updated: 23 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information Zest ("we", "us", "our") collects when you use the Zest mobile app, how we use it, and the choices you have. If you have questions, email zest.privacy@gmail.com.

1. Who we are

Zest is a recipe-keeping and social cooking app operated by Reto Husmann (Switzerland). The app is published as app.zest.mobile on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you give us

  • Account info — email address, password (hashed by our auth provider), username, optional display name and avatar image.
  • Profile preferences — diet (omnivore / vegetarian / vegan), allergens, language preference, account privacy setting (public or private), notification preferences.
  • Phone number (optional) — if you choose to be findable by friends through their contacts. We do not store your phone number in plain text. Before storage we apply SHA-256 with a server-side secret pepper, so the value in our database cannot be reversed back to the original number.
  • Recipes and cook posts — the recipes you save, edit, or import (including URLs and images you provide), cook posts you create, comments you leave, photos you attach, and any collections you organize them into.
  • Follow relationships — who you follow and who follows you, including pending follow requests for private accounts.
  • Recipe history — which recipes you have marked as "cooked", "not yet", or "always good", and how often you've cooked them.
  • Achievements — the in-app badges you have unlocked.

2.2 Information we collect automatically

  • Device identifiers — when you grant permission for push notifications, we store an Expo push token tied to your account so we can send you the notifications you've opted into.
  • Session metadata — your authentication provider (Supabase Auth) records standard sign-in events (timestamp, IP address, user-agent) for security and abuse prevention.
  • Crash and diagnostic data — limited error logs generated by the app when something fails. We do not currently use a third-party analytics SDK.

2.3 Information processed but not stored

  • Contacts (find-friends feature) — if you choose to find friends from your phone's address book, the phone numbers from your contacts are normalized to E.164 format on your device, hashed on our server using the same pepper described above, and matched against the hashes of other Zest users who chose to be findable. We do not store the contacts you uploaded; only the matches that came back to you are returned. The list of contacts you uploaded is discarded after the match completes.
  • Photos for recipe extraction — when you import a recipe by photographing a cookbook or screen, the image is sent to a large-language-model provider (Anthropic or OpenAI, depending on configuration) to extract recipe text. The extracted recipe is stored on your account; the original image is processed transiently and is not retained by us beyond the extraction step unless you explicitly attach it to a cook post.

3. How we use information

We use the information above to:

  • create and authenticate your account,
  • show you your recipes, cook posts, and the content of people you follow,
  • match you with friends from your contacts when you ask us to,
  • send push and in-app notifications you have opted into,
  • enforce our community standards and respond to reports of abuse,
  • diagnose crashes and improve reliability,
  • comply with legal obligations and protect against fraud.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your data to train AI models.

4. Sharing with third parties

We share information only with the service providers we need to run Zest:

  • Supabase, Inc. — our database, authentication, and storage provider. Hosted in Zurich, Switzerland.
  • Apple, Inc. and Google LLC — when you sign in with Apple or Google, those providers share a stable identifier and your email with us.
  • Expo (650 Industries, Inc.) — push notification delivery and OTA update delivery.
  • Anthropic, PBC and/or OpenAI, OpCo, LLC — recipe extraction from images and URLs you import.
  • Google Cloud (Vision API) — automated moderation of images you upload.

These providers process data on our behalf under their own privacy commitments. We do not provide them with content beyond what is necessary for the specific feature in question.

We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Zest, our users, or others.

5. Where your data is stored

Our database and file storage are hosted in Zurich, Switzerland (Supabase Switzerland region). Some processing happens in the United States when we use the third-party APIs listed in section 4 (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google Cloud Vision). Switzerland is recognized by the European Commission as providing an adequate level of data protection, so transfers between the EEA and Switzerland do not require additional safeguards. Transfers to the United States rely on the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework and the equivalent Swiss–U.S. arrangement where the provider is certified, or on Standard Contractual Clauses otherwise. By using Zest you consent to your data being transferred to and processed in these locations.

6. How long we keep your data

We keep your account and the content you create for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account (Settings → "Delete account"), we permanently remove your profile, recipes, cook posts, collections, follows, achievements, and all other data linked to you. This deletion is propagated immediately through our database via cascading foreign-key deletes. Backups are rotated within 30 days, after which deleted data is no longer recoverable.

Hashed phone numbers, if you previously enabled the find-friends feature, are removed at the same time.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you,
  • correct inaccurate data,
  • delete your data (you can do this yourself from Settings; for help with anything outside the in-app flow, email us),
  • export your data,
  • object to or restrict certain processing,
  • withdraw consent for processing that relies on consent (e.g. push notifications, contact matching) — withdrawal does not affect processing that already happened,
  • lodge a complaint with your data-protection authority.

To exercise these rights outside the in-app flow, email zest.privacy@gmail.com. We'll respond within 30 days.

8. Children

Zest is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please email us and we will delete it.

9. Security

We use TLS for all data in transit, modern hashed-password storage for authentication, row-level security on every database table to enforce access at the row level, and a secret server-side pepper for phone-number hashing. No system is perfectly secure; we ask you to use a unique password and to report anything suspicious.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we'll update the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be communicated in the app before they take effect.
